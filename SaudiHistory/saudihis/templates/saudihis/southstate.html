South region

The southern region of Saudi Arabia is home to a plethora of historical sites that provide a captivating glimpse into the region's rich past. One notable landmark is the ancient city of Najran, known for its archaeological sites and historic ruins. The city boasts the archaeological site of Al-Ukhdood, with its ancient tombs and remnants of ancient civilizations. Additionally, the city of Abha showcases the picturesque Asir National Park, where visitors can explore ancient rock art and archaeological sites. The region is also dotted with traditional mud-brick villages, such as Rijal Alma, which offer a unique glimpse into the traditional architecture and way of life of the region. Furthermore, the coastal city of Jizan is home to the historic village of Al-Darb, renowned for its historic buildings and vibrant local culture. These historical places in the south region of Saudi Arabia offer a fascinating journey through time, showcasing the region's cultural heritage and historical significance.
