North region

The northern region of Saudi Arabia is home to a plethora of historical sites that provide a captivating glimpse into the region's past. One notable landmark is the ancient city of Al-Ula, known for its remarkable rock formations and intricate tombs carved into the mountains. The UNESCO World Heritage site of Madain Saleh, also known as Al-Hijr, showcases well-preserved Nabatean tombs and ancient archaeological ruins, offering a fascinating insight into the ancient civilizations that once thrived in the area. Additionally, the city of Tabuk is adorned with historical treasures such as the Tabuk Castle, a magnificent fortress that dates back several centuries. The region also boasts the historical site of Al-Jouf, which features ancient ruins, rock art, and the remnants of ancient civilizations. These historical places in the northern region of Saudi Arabia provide a captivating journey through time, preserving the region's rich cultural heritage and historical significance.
