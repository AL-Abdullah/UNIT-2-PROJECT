East Region

The eastern region of Saudi Arabia is brimming with historical treasures that offer a captivating window into its storied past. Among these gems is the Qatif Historical Village, nestled in the city of Qatif, which proudly exhibits traditional Arabian architecture through its narrow alleyways, mud-brick houses, and ancient mosques. Another remarkable landmark is Tarout Castle, a centuries-old fortress on Tarout Island that played a pivotal role in shaping the region's history. Additionally, the majestic Al-Qarah Mountain in Hofuf holds ancient rock carvings and inscriptions that unveil the secrets of civilizations long gone. Furthermore, the UNESCO World Heritage site of Al-Ahsa Oasis beckons visitors with its lush palm groves, time-honored mud-brick structures, and notable landmarks like Al-Qasr Ibrahim and Joatha Mosque. These historical sites in Saudi Arabia's eastern region offer a mesmerizing journey through time, celebrating the region's cultural heritage and historical significance.
