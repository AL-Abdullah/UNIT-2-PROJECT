West region

The western region of Saudi Arabia is steeped in history, with two standout cities that offer a captivating glimpse into the past. Jeddah, a historic city and UNESCO World Heritage site, enchants visitors with its bustling old town, Al-Balad. This vibrant district showcases traditional architecture, narrow alleyways, and iconic landmarks like the Nasseef House. Taif, nestled amidst picturesque mountains, holds historical importance as a summer retreat for the Saudi royal family. The city boasts notable sites such as Shubra Palace, renowned for its beautiful gardens and traditional architecture. Exploring these historical places in the west region of Saudi Arabia provides a fascinating journey through time, immersing visitors in the region's cultural heritage and historical significance.
